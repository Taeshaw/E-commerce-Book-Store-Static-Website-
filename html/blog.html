Book by Book: Lisa Scottoline

Vannessa Cronin | March 29, 2022

Lisa Scottoline understands how to keep her readers on the edge of their seats. In her latest novel, What Happened to the Bennetts, an ordinary suburban family is victimized by a horrific carjacking, sending them on a harrowing journey they never imagined. The emotional interest in the characters, like in all of Scottoline's works, is what makes the story so high-stakes for readers. She creates people that readers feel as if they know as if they are members of their own family. We were looking forward to going through Scottoline's books one by one (the cute dog pic was the cherry on the sundae).

Read ‘em and weep

Al Woodworth | March 24, 2022

I've previously admitted to having a weakness for books that make me weep. And I'm sure others do as well: who doesn't want to be emotionally invested in the novel they're reading or the characters they're inhabiting? So, here's a list of books that will make you feel everything—fiction and nonfiction, old and fresh. Rest assured that, while they may make you cry, they also confirm humanity's power and may even make you grin. (And if you enjoy the bittersweet—that is, absorbing both the good and the bad—mark your calendars for Susan Cain's new nonfiction book, Bittersweet: How Sorrow and Longing Make Us Whole, which will be released on April 5.)
